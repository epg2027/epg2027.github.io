About EPG

The EPG network provides a platform for scholars studying environmental issues to present research, exchange ideas, and collaborate. While many EPG scholars are trained as political scientists, EPG welcomes scholars from any discipline who work on the issues of environmental politics, policy, or governance.

About the conference

The Call for Papers will be out in October-November with the submission deadline in January. Each proposal is reviewed (blindly) by two members of the international steering committee. By design, the annual conferences are limited to about 60 participants. Typically, conference participants are expected to sign up to provide written feedback on two papers. There are no parallel sessions; all participants attend all panels. Sessions are marked by high-level of audience involvement. Local hosts coordinate the organisation of the conference, draft the programme, and organise keynote speeches, receptions and social activities that provide further opportunities for participants to interact with each other.
